Does the record support temporal proximity for the retaliation claim?

Yes. The internal EEO complaint was filed January 12, 2026 (Complaint p.5). The promotion denial followed 22 days later on February 3, 2026 (Complaint p.7). Termination occurred 62 days after the complaint on March 15, 2026 (Exhibit C). Courts applying Title VII have found temporal proximity of less than three months sufficient to establish a prima facie retaliation case. The 22-day gap between the complaint and the promotion denial is particularly strong on this element.

What does R. Chen's deposition say about who made the final promotion decision?

Chen confirms the hiring committee met January 24, 2026 and that K. Johnson (Hiring Manager) made the final selection recommendation (Dep. R. Chen p.14). Chen states she was not aware of the charging party’s internal EEO complaint at the time of the committee meeting (Dep. R. Chen p.17). This creates a potential conflict with her stated lack of knowledge.

Yes. Williams’ deposition identifies T.
