Case OVP-2026-0034 involves three OPM regulatory citations:

5 CFR Part 831 (CSRS) — Employees hired before January 1, 1984 are generally covered under CSRS. Those with prior military service who elected to deposit the civilian service equivalent are eligible for CSRS Offset (Code K) rather than standard FERS. The retirement plan code on the SF-50 must match the system under which the annuity is computed.

5 CFR 831.102 — Service Computation Date — The SCD must reflect all creditable service including verified military service periods. Per 5 CFR 831.301, military service credit requires an SF-180 from the National Personnel Records Center prior to retirement processing.

OPM Retirement and Insurance Policy Memo 2019-02 — CSRS Offset employees receive a higher annuity base that is partially offset by Social Security benefits at age 62. Computing the annuity at the FERS rate understates the correct benefit and constitutes an improper payment under 31 U.S.C. 3321.
